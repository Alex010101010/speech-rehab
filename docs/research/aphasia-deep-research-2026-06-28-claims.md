### https://www.aphasia.com/aphasia-library/aphasia-treatments/constraint-induced/  
*качество: secondary · утверждений: 5*

- **[central]** Constraint-Induced Language Therapy (now called Language action therapy / CIAT) requires the person with aphasia to communicate only by speaking, forbidding compensatory strategies like drawing, gesturing, or pointing.
  - цитата: «Language action therapy requires the person with aphasia to communicate only by speaking. It does not allow any means of communicating other than verbally.»
- **[central]** CILT rests on three principles: constraint (no compensatory strategies), forced use (talking is the only means of communication), and massed practice (therapy must last a sufficient amount of time).
  - цитата: «Three Principles of language action therapy: Constraint: avoiding the use of any compensatory strategies ... Forced use: talking is the only means of communication ... Massed practice: therapy must last for a sufficient amount of time»
- **[supporting]** CILT is an intensive treatment typically delivered for a few hours a day, ideally every day, not all day every day.
  - цитата: «Because language action therapy is an intense treatment option involving communication, it is not recommended for use all day, every day. It is typically used for a few hours a day, ideally every day.»
- **[supporting]** CILT is suited to people who already have some functional verbal speech; those with severe impairments and no verbal speech may experience frustration with it.
  - цитата: «It is typically used with people who do have some functional verbal speech. People with severe impairments who do not have any verbal speech might experience frustration with this treatment technique.»
- **[supporting]** In Melodic Intonation Therapy the patient is encouraged to tap a finger and slow down speech, and the melody is gradually faded out so speech resembles typical speech as the patient improves.
  - цитата: «The person with aphasia is often encouraged to tap their finger and slow down speech. As the person with aphasia improves, longer sentences will be used. In addition, the melody will be faded out so that it resembles typical speech.»
